# Mass Mortality

## Overview

Mass mortality is an emergency event where bird deaths rise rapidly above normal baseline and threaten immediate farm viability. Sudden mortality spikes can be caused by infectious outbreaks, severe environmental stress, feed or water contamination, or management failures. You should treat any unexplained high-mortality event as a critical incident until proven otherwise. Fast containment, rapid documentation, and urgent veterinary notification are necessary to reduce additional losses and prevent spread.

## Warning Signs

- Daily mortality suddenly increases far above expected flock trend
- Dead birds appear in multiple houses within a short period
- Birds collapse with severe respiratory distress or neurological signs
- Widespread depression, feed refusal, and rapid dehydration
- Simultaneous drop in water intake and production performance
- Strong possibility of exposure to toxic feed, water, or gases
- Mortality continues rising despite immediate routine interventions

## Emergency Response

1. Activate farm emergency response and stop all non-essential movement.
2. Isolate affected houses and enforce strict clean-to-dirty workflow.
3. Contact veterinarian and official veterinary services immediately.
4. Secure feed and water sources for urgent quality assessment.
5. Record mortality by house, age, and time to support rapid investigation.
6. Preserve representative dead birds for veterinary post-mortem sampling.
7. Reinforce personal protective equipment and entry hygiene for all responders.

## Do Not

- Do not move live birds or carcasses off-farm without official guidance.
- Do not dispose of all dead birds before diagnostic sampling is arranged.
- Do not continue using suspect feed or water sources without assessment.
- Do not allow unauthorized access to affected poultry zones.
- Do not ignore rapid mortality progression while waiting for routine schedules.
