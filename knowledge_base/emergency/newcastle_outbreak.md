# Newcastle Outbreak

## Overview

Newcastle outbreak response is an emergency protocol for suspected or confirmed rapid-spread Newcastle disease events with high flock losses. Virulent Newcastle disease can affect an entire unvaccinated flock within days and spread to nearby farms through people, equipment, and bird movement. Immediate containment and reporting are essential to reduce transmission and protect regional poultry production. This guidance provides deterministic first-response actions while veterinary authorities assess and direct next steps.

## Warning Signs

- Sudden rise in mortality over one to three days
- Combined respiratory, neurological, and digestive signs in one flock
- Twisted neck, paralysis, tremors, or circling behaviour
- Gasping, coughing, and noisy breathing in many birds
- Green watery diarrhea and severe weakness
- Sharp egg production drop and shell quality decline
- Fast spread of signs across pens or houses

## Emergency Response

1. Isolate affected birds and stop all flock movement on and off the farm.
2. Restrict entry to essential staff only and enforce entry disinfection points.
3. Notify your veterinarian and local veterinary authority immediately.
4. Separate tools, clothing, and footwear for affected and unaffected areas.
5. Remove dead birds promptly and hold for official disposal instructions.
6. Record house-level mortality, clinical signs, and timeline of spread.
7. Reinforce strict biosecurity until official clearance is provided.

## Do Not

- Do not sell birds, eggs, or manure during the outbreak period.
- Do not move birds between pens or houses.
- Do not share equipment with nearby farms.
- Do not delay veterinary notification while attempting informal treatment.
- Do not allow uncontrolled visitor or vehicle entry to poultry areas.
