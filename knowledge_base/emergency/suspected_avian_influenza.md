# Suspected Avian Influenza

## Overview

Suspected Avian Influenza is a critical farm emergency because highly pathogenic strains can spread quickly and cause very high mortality in poultry. The condition is notifiable and requires immediate reporting to veterinary authorities. Delay in response increases risk to nearby farms and raises public health risk for people with close exposure to sick or dead birds. This document provides immediate, deterministic response actions you should follow before laboratory confirmation.

## Warning Signs

- Sudden death of multiple birds with little or no previous signs
- Rapid spread of severe illness across one or more houses
- Swollen head, face, comb, or wattles
- Purple-blue discoloration of comb, wattles, or legs
- Severe respiratory distress, gasping, or open-mouth breathing
- Sharp egg production drop in layers within one to two days
- Neurological signs such as tremors, twisted neck, or incoordination

## Emergency Response

1. Stop all movement of birds, eggs, feed, equipment, and vehicles immediately.
2. Isolate the affected house and restrict all non-essential farm entry.
3. Notify your local veterinarian or official veterinary authority at once.
4. Use gloves, face mask, and protective clothing for any unavoidable handling.
5. Separate healthy and sick areas with strict clean-to-dirty movement control.
6. Record mortality counts, affected houses, and time of first observed signs.
7. Follow official instructions for sampling, quarantine, disposal, and disinfection.

## Do Not

- Do not sell, move, or slaughter birds from the suspected farm.
- Do not consume or distribute eggs or carcasses from affected houses.
- Do not allow visitors, traders, or transporters into poultry areas.
- Do not hide mortality data or delay reporting while waiting for confirmation.
- Do not mix equipment between affected and unaffected houses without disinfection.
