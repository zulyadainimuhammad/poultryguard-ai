# FAQ: Diseases

## Question

How can I quickly recognize a poultry disease problem and know when to call for help?

## Answer

Watch your flock at least twice daily for sudden changes in behavior, feeding, breathing, droppings, and mortality. Early warning signs include reduced feed intake, lethargy, coughing, diarrhea, egg drop, and unexplained deaths. If signs spread quickly across houses or mortality rises above your normal baseline, treat it as an urgent event and contact a veterinarian immediately. Isolate visibly sick birds, restrict movement of birds and equipment, and keep clear records of when signs started. Fast reporting and early containment reduce losses and help prevent spread to nearby farms.
