# FAQ: Housing

## Question

What housing checks should I do every day to prevent heat, cold, and ventilation problems?

## Answer

Inspect temperature, airflow, litter dryness, and bird distribution in each house every day. Birds that crowd in one area, pant heavily, or huddle tightly are strong warning signs of poor housing conditions. Keep ventilation active, remove wet litter quickly, and adjust curtains or inlets based on weather changes. Ensure water systems work well because heat stress and dehydration can escalate fast. Daily housing checks reduce mortality risk and support steady growth and egg production.
