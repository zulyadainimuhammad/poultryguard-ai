# Production Records

## Overview

Production records are the routine performance data you collect to track how efficiently your flock is converting feed, water, and management inputs into meat or eggs. These records include growth, egg output, feed use, water use, mortality, and culls. Consistent production tracking helps you identify weak performance early and improve decisions on nutrition, health, and housing. Strong records turn daily farm activities into measurable results you can manage and improve. This evidence-based approach supports faster correction when performance starts to decline.

## Key Information

### Why It Matters

Production performance changes gradually, and without records the underlying cause is often missed. Reliable records help you compare current results with targets and previous cycles. This makes it easier to detect costly issues such as poor feed efficiency, egg decline, and high cull rates. Production records also support budgeting, forecasting, and farm expansion planning.

### Causes of Weak Production Tracking

- Incomplete daily data capture
- No consistent units for feed, water, and output
- Missing linkage between production and health events
- Irregular weekly performance review
- No baseline targets for comparison

### Risk Factors

- Large flocks managed without standard recording templates
- Frequent staff changes with poor handover
- Manual recording errors and delayed data entry
- No verification of feeder and water meter readings
- Poor storage of previous cycle records

### Clinical or Production Impacts

- Reduced ability to detect falling performance quickly
- Higher feed cost from unrecognized inefficiency
- Delayed correction of egg production decline
- Poor planning for market timing and sales volume
- Repeated management errors across production cycles

### Prevention

Use one standardized production sheet for each flock and update it daily. Include key indicators such as feed intake, water intake, average body weight or egg count, mortality, and culls. Review weekly trends and compare with planned targets for age and production stage. Keep historical cycle data for benchmarking and improvement planning.

### Monitoring

Track and review core production indicators routinely.

- Daily feed and water consumption per flock
- Weekly body weight for broilers or production percentage for layers
- Egg count, egg weight, and shell quality for layers
- Feed conversion trend and cumulative mortality
- Market-age performance versus planned target

## Recommendations

### Farmer Guidance

1. Record production data every day without skipping.
2. Use consistent units for all measurements.
3. Compare weekly performance against targets and prior cycles.
4. Investigate major deviations within 24 to 48 hours.
5. Integrate production records with health and treatment logs.
6. Share summaries with technical advisers during routine reviews.
7. Use record trends to guide feed, housing, and stocking decisions.

### Emergency Warning Signs

Respond urgently when feed use rises but growth or egg output falls, when water intake changes sharply without weather explanation, or when production drops together with increasing mortality. Immediate investigation is required if multiple key indicators deteriorate in the same week. Fast corrective action can prevent full-cycle performance failure.

### Practical Checklist

- [ ] Daily feed and water records are complete
- [ ] Growth or egg production indicators are updated on schedule
- [ ] Mortality and culls are linked to production data
- [ ] Weekly target comparison is completed
- [ ] Major deviations are investigated promptly
- [ ] Historical cycle records are stored and accessible
- [ ] Improvement actions are documented and reviewed
