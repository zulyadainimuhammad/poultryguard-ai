# Broiler Vaccination Schedule

## Overview

A broiler vaccination schedule is the planned sequence of vaccines given to broiler chickens from hatch to market. Broilers grow rapidly (market weight at 6–7 weeks) and require fast, reliable immunity. Vaccination protects against major respiratory viruses (Newcastle, Infectious Bronchitis), immunosuppressive viruses (Gumboro), and bacterial pathogens. This schedule is designed for smallholder farms in Africa where disease pressure is high and access to veterinary care is limited.

## Key Information

### Purpose of Broiler Vaccination

Broiler vaccination achieves four goals:

1. **Rapid immunity** — chickens must be protected within 3–4 days of hatch before virus exposure
2. **Mucosal protection** — vaccines given via water or eye drop protect respiratory surfaces where viruses enter
3. **Systemic protection** — vaccines given by injection provide deep, lasting immunity
4. **Economical production** — prevents mortality, condemnation, and production loss; vaccine cost is far less than disease losses

### Recommended Broiler Vaccination Schedule

This schedule applies to commercial and smallholder broiler flocks in sub-Saharan Africa.

| Age | Vaccine | Disease | Route | Quantity per 1,000 birds | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Day 0 (hatch) | Marek (HVT or SB-1) | Marek's disease | In ovo or spray | 1,000 doses | Done at hatchery; verify documentation |
| Day 0 (hatch) | Newcastle (La Sota) | Newcastle disease | Eye drop or spray | 1,000 doses | May be combined with Marek |
| Day 7 | Newcastle (La Sota) booster | Newcastle disease | Drinking water | 10 litres of water + vaccine | Ensure all chicks drink |
| Day 10 | Gumboro (IBD) | Infectious Bursal Disease | Drinking water | 10 litres of water + vaccine | Timing critical: delay causes vaccine failure |
| Day 14 | Newcastle (La Sota) booster | Newcastle disease | Drinking water | 10 litres of water + vaccine | Optional if water vaccination at day 7 was strong |
| Day 21 | Infectious Bronchitis (IB) | Infectious Bronchitis | Drinking water or spray | 10 litres of water + vaccine | May omit if IB is not endemic in region |

### Vaccine Types and Characteristics

**Newcastle Disease Vaccines (Lentogenic strains):**
- La Sota (most common in Africa)
- B1 strain
- Clone 30 and V4 strains
- All are live attenuated; provide mucosal immunity
- Compatible with Marek's vaccine at hatch

**Marek's Disease Vaccines:**
- Herpesvirus of Turkey (HVT) — most common, safe, effective
- Strain SB-1 (also common)
- Strain CVI988 (used in some regions)
- Given at hatchery; do not vaccinate after hatch
- No "booster" for Marek

**Gumboro (IBD) Vaccines:**
- Live attenuated vaccines (various strains)
- Timing critical: too early (high maternal antibodies block vaccine), too late (infection occurs)
- Day 10 is optimal for most commercial breeds
- Local timing may vary; consult veterinarian

**Infectious Bronchitis Vaccines:**
- Live attenuated strains (Mass, Connecticut, etc.)
- Only use if IB is known to be present in region
- May be omitted in low-disease-pressure areas

### Administration Routes

**Eye Drop (Eye Instillation):**
- Most reliable for individual immunity
- Labor-intensive; mainly used at hatchery
- One drop in one eye; bird shakes head and virus reaches respiratory tract

**Spray Vaccination:**
- For large groups at hatchery or on-farm
- Aerosol mist; birds inhale vaccine
- Fast, but variable coverage; requires proper equipment

**Drinking Water (Most Common for Farmers):**
- Add vaccine to water; birds drink within 2 hours
- Requires removal of all water sources for 2 hours before vaccination
- Stop feed (optional) to increase thirst
- Ensure all birds drink; watch for non-drinkers
- Replace water after 2 hours; discard any remaining vaccinated water
- Cannot use chlorinated water or water with mineral deposits

**Feed (Rare):**
- Some Newcastle vaccines available in feed form
- Less reliable than water or eye drop; avoid if possible

## Recommendations

Follow these steps for successful broiler vaccination:

1. **Verify hatchery vaccination** — obtain documentation that day-0 vaccines were given
2. **Plan vaccination dates** — mark calendar for each vaccination
3. **Check vaccine cold chain** — store at 2–8 °C until use; never freeze; use within hours of opening
4. **Prepare water supply** — remove all drinkers 2 hours before scheduled vaccination
5. **Calculate vaccine volume** — order enough for all birds; buy fresh vaccine
6. **Mix vaccine correctly** — follow label instructions; use clean, unchlorinated water
7. **Apply vaccine** — add to water; ensure all birds drink within 2 hours
8. **Watch for side effects** — mild ruffling, reduced appetite for 24 hours is normal
9. **Keep records** — note dates, vaccine batch numbers, number of birds vaccinated
10. **Plan for disease peaks** — expect temporary mild signs 5–7 days post-vaccination; this is normal

## When to Call a Veterinarian

Contact a veterinarian if you observe:

- Severe depression or paralysis after vaccination (vaccine reaction)
- More than 5% mortality within 5 days of vaccination
- Failure to improve 2 weeks after final vaccination
- Unexpected respiratory disease despite vaccination
- Vaccines unavailable or expired

**Always consult a licensed veterinarian before administering any drug or vaccine. The guidance in this document is for general awareness only and does not replace professional veterinary advice.**

### Emergency Actions

If vaccination reactions occur:

1. Remove remaining vaccinated water immediately
2. Provide clean, fresh water with electrolytes
3. Stop feed for 24 hours if severe
4. Ensure good ventilation
5. Do not vaccinate again until birds recover
6. Call veterinarian if birds do not improve within 48 hours

### Farmer Checklist

Use this checklist for broiler vaccination:

- [ ] Hatchery documentation for day-0 vaccines received and filed
- [ ] Vaccination schedule written on farm calendar
- [ ] Vaccines ordered and stored at 2–8 °C
- [ ] Water drinkers removed 2 hours before scheduled vaccination
- [ ] Vaccine mixed in unchlorinated water
- [ ] All birds observed drinking vaccinated water
- [ ] Records kept: date, vaccine type, batch number, birds vaccinated
- [ ] Birds observed for normal post-vaccination signs (mild ruffling day 1)
- [ ] No unexpected disease 2 weeks post-vaccination
- [ ] Market birds processed after day 21 (if withdrawal period required)
