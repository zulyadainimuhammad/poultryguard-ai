# Layer Vaccination Schedule

## Overview

A layer vaccination schedule protects laying hens and breeders throughout their productive lives (18+ months). Unlike broilers, layers require durable immunity maintained by periodic boosters. Layers face viral diseases (Newcastle, Infectious Bronchitis, Gumboro), bacterial pathogens, and reproductive diseases. A comprehensive vaccination program ensures consistent egg production, food safety (no contaminated eggs), and long flock lifespan.

## Key Information

### Purpose of Layer Vaccination

Layer vaccination achieves five critical goals:

1. **Long-lasting immunity** — 70+ week production life requires sustained protection
2. **Mucosal protection** — respiratory viruses prevented at point of entry
3. **Systemic protection** — deep immunity from injection vaccines
4. **Reproductive health** — prevents viral contamination of eggs
5. **Food safety** — ensures eggs are free of Salmonella and other pathogens

### Recommended Layer Vaccination Schedule

This schedule applies to replacement pullets (young hens) and laying flocks.

#### Chick Period (0–16 weeks)

| Age | Vaccine | Disease | Route | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Day 0 (hatch) | Marek (HVT or SB-1) | Marek's disease | In ovo or spray | Done at hatchery; verify documentation |
| Day 0 (hatch) | Newcastle (La Sota) | Newcastle disease | Eye drop or spray | May be combined with Marek |
| Day 7 | Newcastle (La Sota) booster | Newcastle disease | Drinking water | Ensure all chicks drink |
| Day 10 | Gumboro (IBD) | Infectious Bursal Disease | Drinking water | Timing critical; delay causes failure |
| Day 21 | Infectious Bronchitis (IB) | Infectious Bronchitis | Drinking water or spray | Optional if IB not endemic |
| Week 8–10 | Pullorum-Typhoid test or vaccine | Bacterial diseases | Blood test (test) or vaccination | Test broiler breeders; vaccinate if positive history |
| Week 10–12 | Newcastle inactivated vaccine | Newcastle disease | Intramuscular injection | Provides longer-lasting immunity for layers |
| Week 12–16 | Bronchitis inactivated vaccine | Infectious Bronchitis | Intramuscular injection | For endemic regions; boosters immunity |

#### Grower Period (16–16 weeks before lay)

| Age | Vaccine | Disease | Route | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 14–16 | Newcastle (La Sota) booster | Newcastle disease | Drinking water | If live vaccine needs refreshing |
| Week 16–18 | Salmonella typhimurium vaccine | Salmonella | Intramuscular injection | Important for export or safety programs |

#### Prelay/Breeder Period (16–20 weeks before first egg)

| Age | Vaccine | Disease | Route | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Week 18–20 | Newcastle inactivated (oil-emulsion) | Newcastle disease | Intramuscular injection | Durable immunity for 12 months |
| Week 20 | Infectious Bronchitis inactivated booster | Infectious Bronchitis | Intramuscular injection | If previously vaccinated; only if endemic |
| Week 20 | Gumboro inactivated booster | Gumboro/IBD | Intramuscular injection | Optional; enhances gut immunity |

#### During Lay (First Year)

| Age | Vaccine | Disease | Route | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Month 3 post-lay | Newcastle inactivated booster | Newcastle disease | Intramuscular injection | If oil-emulsion vaccine used; boost at 3–4 months |
| Month 8–10 post-lay | Newcastle inactivated booster | Newcastle disease | Intramuscular injection | Annual boost; prevents outbreak |

#### Second Year Lay (If Retained)

| Timing | Vaccine | Disease | Route | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Before 16-month mark | Newcastle live (La Sota) | Newcastle disease | Drinking water | Refreshes mucosal immunity |
| Before 18-month mark | Newcastle inactivated | Newcastle disease | Intramuscular injection | Maintains systemic immunity |
| Before lay resumes | Infectious Bronchitis live | Infectious Bronchitis | Drinking water | If IB remains endemic |

### Vaccine Types for Layers

**Live Attenuated Vaccines:**
- Newcastle La Sota (all ages)
- Infectious Bronchitis live strains
- Gumboro live strains (younger birds)
- Fast-acting; mucosal immunity; less cost
- Limited duration (weeks to months)

**Inactivated (Killed) Vaccines:**
- Newcastle oil-emulsion injected vaccine
- Infectious Bronchitis inactivated
- Gumboro inactivated (older birds)
- Longer immunity (12+ months)
- More expensive; require injection
- Ideal for long-term layer protection

**Combination Vaccines:**
- Newcastle + Bronchitis inactivated (single injection)
- Reduces number of injections
- Verify compatibility; do not mix without veterinary guidance

### Administration Routes

**Drinking Water:**
- Best for live vaccines in chicks and growers
- As described in Broiler Vaccination Schedule
- Remove drinkers 2 hours before vaccination

**Intramuscular Injection:**
- For inactivated vaccines in pullets and layers
- Inject into breast muscle or thigh
- One dose per bird; 0.3–0.5 mL typical
- Requires trained personnel
- Risk of injection site swelling, abscess if contaminated

**Intranasal/Eye Drop:**
- At hatchery only
- Not performed by farmers

## Recommendations

Follow these steps for successful layer vaccination:

1. **Verify hatchery documentation** — ensure day-0 vaccines were given and recorded
2. **Plan pullet vaccination timeline** — schedule all shots from 0‖20 weeks
3. **Arrange injection services** — find trained person for inactivated vaccines (weeks 10+)
4. **Monitor pullet health** — watch for illness before pullet vaccination; vaccinate only healthy birds
5. **Store vaccines properly** — live vaccines at 2–8 °C; inactivated at 2–8 °C; never freeze
6. **Keep detailed records** — vaccine type, date, batch number, reaction observed
7. **Plan breeder vaccination** — if keeping a breeding flock, vaccinate for Salmonella
8. **Maintain booster schedule** — during lay, do not skip annual Newcastle boosters
9. **Do not vaccinate during lay**  — avoid water vaccination during peak lay; use injection if needed
10. **Consult veterinarian** — confirm schedule appropriate for your region and disease situation

## When to Call a Veterinarian

Contact a veterinarian if you observe:

- Injection site swelling, redness, or abscess formation
- Severe depression, paralysis, or death after vaccination
- Unexpected illness 2 weeks after final pullet vaccination
- Vaccination reaction spreading to other pullets
- Sudden egg production drop after vaccination
- Signs of Marek's disease (paralysis) despite vaccination

**Always consult a licensed veterinarian before administering any drug or vaccine. The guidance in this document is for general awareness only and does not replace professional veterinary advice.**

### Emergency Actions

If vaccination reactions occur:

1. Stop vaccination immediately
2. Provide clean water with electrolytes
3. Ensure good ventilation
4. Monitor for spread to other birds
5. Isolate severely affected birds
6. Do not vaccinate again until birds recover
7. Call veterinarian if reaction worsens

### Farmer Checklist

Use this checklist for layer vaccination:

- [ ] Day-0 vaccine documentation received from hatchery
- [ ] Layer vaccination schedule written on calendar (weeks 0–20)
- [ ] Live vaccines stored at 2–8 °C; backup power for refrigeration arranged
- [ ] Water vaccination performed on schedule (weeks 7, 10, 21)
- [ ] Injection services arranged for weeks 10–20
- [ ] Trained person identified for inactivated vaccine injections
- [ ] Syringes, needles, alcohol wipes prepared before injection day
- [ ] Injection sites (thigh/breast) selected and marked
- [ ] Injection reactions monitored for 24 hours
- [ ] Vaccination records kept: date, vaccine, batch, number treated, reactions
- [ ] Annual Newcastle booster given during lay (months 8–10)
- [ ] No unexpected disease outbreak post-vaccination
- [ ] Eggs tested negative for Salmonella (if applicable)
