# Vaccine Administration Techniques

## Overview

Vaccine effectiveness depends on proper administration technique. Even high-quality, properly stored vaccines fail if given incorrectly. Common mistakes include using chlorinated water, injecting incorrectly, using incorrect needle size, or vaccinating sick birds. This document explains five administration routes, how to perform each correctly, common mistakes to avoid, and what to do if vaccinated animals show adverse reactions.

## Key Information

### Drinking Water Vaccination (Most Common for Farmers)

**Purpose:** Deliver live vaccine to all birds simultaneously; vaccines reach respiratory and digestive surfaces.

**When to Use:**
- Broilers and layers: weeks 1, 2, 3 (live Newcastle, Gumboro, Bronchitis)
- Hatchery: rarely performed by farmers (done at hatchery)
- Emergency vaccination: rapid protection for outbreaks

**Equipment:**
- Clean water containers (free of algae, mineral deposits, disinfectant)
- Measuring cup or syringe for vaccine volume
- Clean mixing spoon or stick
- Thermometer (to verify water not too cold)

**Procedure:**

1. **Prepare birds (2 hours before):** Remove all water sources; stop feed (optional but increases thirst)
2. **Prepare water:** Use fresh, unchlorinated water at room temperature (20–25 °C)
   - Do NOT use chlorinated water (kills vaccine virus)
   - Do NOT use mineral-rich water (causes clumping)
   - Filter water if visibly cloudy
3. **Calculate volume:** For 1,000 birds, use 10 litres of water + vaccine
   - Adjust proportionally for fewer birds
4. **Mix vaccine:** Add vaccine vial to water; stir gently for 2–3 minutes
   - Do not shake vigorously (damages virus)
   - Ensure even distribution; do not leave vaccine sitting at bottom
5. **Provide vaccinated water:** Place containers where birds can drink easily
   - Ensure all birds access water within 2 hours
   - Watch for non-drinkers; use smaller containers if needed
6. **Monitor drinking:** Most birds drink within 30 minutes
   - Return after 1 hour; add more water if needed
   - Return after 2 hours; remove all remaining vaccinated water
7. **Return normal water:** After 2 hours, replace with fresh, unchlorinated water
   - Keep replacement water available at all times

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**

- Using chlorinated water (kills vaccine)
- Water too hot (> 30 °C) or too cold (< 10 °C)
- Not removing water sources 2 hours before (birds not thirsty; do not drink vaccine)
- Shaking or vigorously mixing (damages virus)
- Using mineral-rich water (causes vaccine clumping)
- Leaving vaccinated water longer than 2 hours (vaccine expires)
- Vaccinating sick or stressed birds (poor immune response)

**Success Indicators:**

- All birds drink within 2 hours
- Water consumption normal throughout vaccination period
- Mild ruffling or reduced appetite for 24 hours (normal)
- No unusual mortality (> 1% excess)
- Flock appears healthy 1 week post-vaccination

### Intramuscular Injection (For Inactivated Vaccines)

**Purpose:** Deliver inactivated (killed) vaccine to systemic circulation; produces long-lasting immunity.

**When to Use:**
- Layers: weeks 10, 12, 18–20 (Newcastle, Bronchitis inactivated vaccines)
- Older broilers: only if vaccination program extended
- Never for live vaccines (waste of material)

**Equipment:**
- Sterile syringe (1 mL typical)
- Sterile needle (24–26 gauge, 5/8” length for chicks; 18–20 gauge, 1” for adult birds)
- Alcohol or povidone-iodine for skin antisepsis
- Clean cloth or wipes
- Container for used needles (sharps disposal)

**Procedure:**

1. **Prepare vaccine:** Remove from refrigerator 30 minutes before injection (allow to reach room temperature)
   - Check expiration date; check for cracks or leaks
   - Gently invert bottle 5‐6 times (do not shake if oil-emulsion; do not foment separation)
2. **Prepare syringe:** Draw up correct dose (typically 0.3–0.5 mL per bird; verify label)
   - Use sterile technique; do not touch needle tip
3. **Restrain bird:** Hold gently but firmly; do not crush wings
4. **Prepare injection site:** Choose thigh muscle (medial thigh, inside upper leg) or breast muscle
   - Thigh: safer for untrained personnel; less visible
   - Breast: common commercial site; risk of injection site reactions
   - Clean area with alcohol wipe; allow to dry
5. **Inject vaccine:**
   - Insert needle into muscle at slight angle (30–45°)
   - Needle should insert 5–10 mm below skin surface
   - Aspirate (pull back plunger slightly) to check for blood vessel (rare but possible)
   - Inject vaccine slowly (over 2–3 seconds); do not rush
   - Remove needle and gently press injection site with cloth
6. **Monitor:** Watch for injection site swelling, abscess, lameness over next 48 hours
   - Minor swelling and tenderness normal for 24–48 hours
   - Severe swelling or abscess indicates infection; call veterinarian

**Common Mistakes to Avoid:**

- Injecting subcutaneously (under skin) instead of intramuscularly (vaccine effectiveness reduced)
- Using dull or bent needles (causes tissue damage, pain)
- Injecting too deeply (risk of organ puncture)
- Injecting when birds are chilled (vaccine efficacy reduced)
- Injecting sick birds (poor immune response)
- Reusing needles without sterilization (infection risk)
- Pulling needle out too quickly (leaking vaccine)
- Not cleaning injection site (infection risk)

**Success Indicators:**

- All birds successfully injected
- Minor swelling at injection site (normal; resolves in 48 hours)
- No lameness or severe swelling
- Flock appears healthy 1 week post-injection
- No abscess formation

### Eye Drop (Hatchery Only; Rare for Farmers)

**Purpose:** Live vaccine applied directly to eye; bird blinks and distributes to respiratory tract.

**When to Use:** Hatchery only; not performed by farmers (day-old chicks).

**If performed on farm:**

1. Restrain chick gently
2. Open eyelid (or use both upper and lower)
3. Place one drop vaccine in corner of eye
4. Release bird; bird will blink and distribute vaccine
5. Do not force eye open (damage)

### Spray Vaccination

**Purpose:** Aerosol mist; birds inhale vaccine; used for large flocks at hatchery.

**When to Use:** Hatchery only; not performed by farmers.

**If attempted on farm:**

1. Use commercial spray equipment (nebulizer)
2. Dilute vaccine according to label (typically 1:1 with diluent)
3. Spray continuously for 1–2 minutes
4. Ensure all birds exposed to mist
5. Variable coverage; less reliable than water or injection

### Feed Vaccination (Rarely Used)

**Purpose:** Vaccine mixed in feed; birds eat and ingest vaccine.

**When to Use:** Only if specified on label; most Newcastle vaccines are water or eye drop only.

**Issues:**
- Poor uptake (birds do not eat enough vaccinated feed)
- Inconsistent coverage
- Not recommended; avoid unless specifically directed

## Recommendations

Follow these steps for safe vaccine administration:

1. **Read label completely** — verify vaccination route, dosage, reconstitution
2. **Verify target birds** — only vaccinate indicated species and age
3. **Use unchlorinated water** — test water quality before vaccination
4. **Vaccinate only healthy birds** — do not vaccinate sick, stressed, or injured birds
5. **Follow timing** — vaccinate on correct date; do not delay or advance
6. **Use sterile technique** — for injections, wear gloves; use clean needles
7. **Monitor immediately post-vaccination** — watch for adverse reactions over 30 minutes
8. **Keep birds calm** — minimize handling stress after vaccination
9. **Document everything** — record vaccine type, date, number vaccinated, batch number, reactions
10. **Report adverse events** — contact veterinarian if unusual reactions occur

## When to Call a Veterinarian

Contact a veterinarian if you observe:

- Severe depression or tremors after vaccination
- Injection site abscess or severe swelling (> 2 cm diameter)
- Lameness lasting > 48 hours post-injection
- Birds unable to walk or stand 24 hours post-vaccination
- Unexpected high mortality (> 2% excess) within 48 hours
- Non-drinkers during water vaccination (unable to consume vaccine)
- Vaccination does not protect (disease outbreak despite vaccination)

**Always consult a licensed veterinarian before administering any drug or vaccine. The guidance in this document is for general awareness only and does not replace professional veterinary advice.**

### Emergency Actions

If adverse reaction occurs:

1. Stop vaccination immediately
2. Provide fresh water with electrolytes
3. Ensure good ventilation and calm environment
4. Isolate severely affected birds
5. Monitor closely for 24 hours
6. Call veterinarian if birds do not improve
7. Do not revaccinate until birds fully recovered and veterinarian approves

### Farmer Checklist

Use this checklist for vaccine administration:

- [ ] Vaccine label read completely; route and dosage confirmed
- [ ] Expiration date verified (vaccine not expired)
- [ ] Birds are healthy; no signs of disease or stress
- [ ] Equipment prepared and clean (syringes, needles, water containers)
- [ ] Water tested (unchlorinated; free of algae/mineral deposits)
- [ ] Vaccination date recorded on calendar and in farm notebook
- [ ] All birds have access to drinking water or injection site ready
- [ ] Vaccine mixed or drawn up correctly per label
- [ ] All birds vaccinated within 2-hour window
- [ ] Remaining vaccinated water discarded after 2 hours
- [ ] Fresh water provided after vaccination period
- [ ] Birds monitored for 24 hours post-vaccination
- [ ] Injection sites checked for swelling or infection (48 hours post-injection)
- [ ] Batch number and number of birds vaccinated recorded
- [ ] Any adverse reactions documented and reported to veterinarian
